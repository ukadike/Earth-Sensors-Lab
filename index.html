Earth Sensors Lab

Environmental observation, accessible STEAM, and living laboratories.

What this is

Earth Sensors Lab is a Small Systems Lab branch for hands-on environmental sensing, garden-based science, astronomy, accessibility, and storytelling.

It helps students and communities observe soil, weather, light, sound, plant life, and sky events through low-cost tools, tactile learning, data sonification, and public-interest science education.

SSL method inside ESL

Operations of Care

What is being sensed? Who owns the data? What should be shared publicly, and what should remain local to the school or community? How do students understand the sensor before trusting the data? How does the project work without perfect internet or expensive equipment?

Rule-Based Intelligence

ESL can use rule-based prompts and simple AI support for sensor anomaly flags, plain-language explanations, classroom observation logs, accessibility summaries, data quality warnings, and "do not overclaim" science checks.

Ancient Geometry

Circle: cycles of day and night, seasons, water, plant growth. Triangle: sensor, environment, human observer. Grid: garden plots, data tables, maps. Spiral: repeated observation and learning over time. Golden ratio: natural growth patterns and visual balance.

Starter modules

Six prototype modules, in development.

  1. Soil moisture and plant care.
  2. Light, heat, and microclimate.
  3. Sound and biodiversity listening.
  4. Weather diary and sky observation.
  5. Astronomy and interstellar object literacy.
  6. Data storytelling and public science.

Accessibility baseline

  • Tactile diagrams.
  • Audio descriptions.
  • Captions and transcripts.
  • Screen-reader-ready pages.
  • High contrast charts.
  • Low-cost hardware assumptions.
  • Offline classroom worksheets.
