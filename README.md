# Earth Sensors Lab

**Environmental observation · Accessible STEAM · Living laboratories**

Earth Sensors Lab is a Small Systems Lab branch for hands-on environmental sensing, garden-based science, astronomy, accessibility, and storytelling.

It helps students and communities observe soil, weather, light, sound, plant life, and sky events through low-cost tools, tactile learning, data sonification, and public-interest science education.

## SSL Method Inside ESL

### Operations of Care
- What is being sensed?
- Who owns the data?
- What should be shared publicly?
- What should remain local to the school or community?
- How do students understand the sensor before trusting the data?
- How does the project work without perfect internet or expensive equipment?

### Rule-Based Intelligence
ESL can use rule-based prompts and simple AI support for:
- sensor anomaly flags;
- plain-language explanations;
- classroom observation logs;
- accessibility summaries;
- data quality warnings;
- “do not overclaim” science checks.

### Ancient Geometry in ESL
- **Circle** — cycles: day/night, seasons, water, plant growth.
- **Triangle** — sensor, environment, human observer.
- **Grid** — garden plots, data tables, maps.
- **Spiral** — repeated observation and learning over time.
- **Golden ratio** — natural growth patterns and visual balance.

## Starter Modules

## Accessibility Baseline

- Tactile diagrams.
- Audio descriptions.
- Captions and transcripts.
- Screen-reader-ready pages.
- High contrast charts.
- Low-cost hardware assumptions.
- Offline classroom worksheets.
